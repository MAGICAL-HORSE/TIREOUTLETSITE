LIFT KIT FAQ

Everything you need to know before lifting your truck

What's the difference between a leveling kit and a lift kit?

A leveling kit only raises the front of your truck 1.5"–3" to match the rear height (correcting the factory rake). A full lift kit raises all four corners, giving you 4"–8"+ of total clearance for bigger tires and serious off-road capability. Leveling kits are less expensive and less involved — full lifts require more parts and labor.

Do I need an alignment after a lift?

Yes — always. Any time you alter suspension geometry, alignment is required. We include an alignment check with every lift kit install to make sure your truck tracks straight and your tires don't wear unevenly.

What size tires can I run after a lift?

It depends on the lift height and your specific vehicle. A leveling kit might let you run 33" tires. A 4" lift typically fits 35"s. A 6" or 8" lift can fit 37"–40"+ depending on the truck. We'll tell you exactly what fits your setup when you call or come in.

How long does a lift kit install take?

A leveling kit is usually done in 2–3 hours. A full suspension lift typically takes a full day depending on the kit and vehicle. Call ahead and we'll give you an accurate timeframe for your specific truck.

Will a lift affect my warranty?

Aftermarket modifications can affect certain vehicle warranties depending on your manufacturer and dealer. We recommend checking your warranty terms. We use quality parts and can discuss your options when you come in.

Can I finance my lift kit?

Yes — we partner with Snap Finance and Acima, both lease-to-own programs that don't require traditional credit. Apply online before you come in or we can walk you through it at the shop.
