That Little Light on Your Dash

When your TPMS warning light turns on, your tire pressure monitoring system has detected a problem — low pressure, a faulty sensor, or a system error. Don't ignore it.

What Is TPMS?

The Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) is a federal safety requirement on all vehicles made after 2007. It monitors the air pressure in each tire in real time and alerts you when pressure drops 25% or more below the recommended level.

A working TPMS can prevent blowouts, improve fuel economy, and extend the life of your tires — especially important on Houston's hot summer roads.

  • Required on all vehicles 2008 and newer
  • Each sensor has a battery that lasts 5–10 years
  • Sensors often need replacement after new tires
  • System must be recalibrated after tire rotation
  • Aftermarket wheels can knock sensors out of sync

WHEN YOUR TPMS LIGHT COMES ON

Don't ignore these warning signs — pull in and let us check it

⚠️

Low Tire Pressure

Most common cause. Pressure dropped below the safe threshold — could be a slow leak, temperature drop, or underinflation.

🪫

Dead Sensor Battery

TPMS sensors run on batteries that typically last 5–10 years. When the battery dies, the sensor needs to be replaced entirely.

🔁

After Tire Rotation

Rotating tires moves sensors to new positions. The system needs to be retrained to recognize sensor locations again.

🛞

New Tires or Rims

New aftermarket wheels may not be compatible with your existing sensors. We make sure everything communicates properly.

❄️

Cold Weather

Every 10°F temperature drop can lower tire pressure by 1–2 PSI. Houston winters won't freeze you, but morning temps still trigger warnings.

COMMON TPMS QUESTIONS

Straight answers from the shop floor

Can I drive with my TPMS light on?

You can, but you shouldn't ignore it. If the light came on because of low pressure, continuing to drive on underinflated tires can damage the tire, ruin the rim, and in extreme cases cause a blowout. Stop by and we'll check it in minutes.

Do I need to replace all 4 sensors at once?

Not necessarily. We can replace just the faulty sensor. However, if your sensors are old (7+ years), replacing all four at once saves time and money in the long run since the others will likely fail soon anyway.

Will new tires affect my TPMS?

Yes — when we mount new tires, the sensors may need to be relearned to their new positions. We handle this as part of the tire installation process so your system works correctly when you leave.

What vehicles do you service TPMS on?

All of them — cars, trucks, SUVs, and vans. Ford, Chevy, Toyota, Honda, RAM, GMC, Dodge, Nissan, Hyundai, and more. If it has a TPMS system, we can work on it.

How long does TPMS service take?

A simple reset or pressure check takes 15–30 minutes. Sensor replacement is usually done within an hour. Walk in any day — no appointment needed.
